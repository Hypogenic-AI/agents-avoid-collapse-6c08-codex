# Downloaded Papers

1. [The Curse of Recursion: Training on Generated Data Makes Models Forget](2305.17493_curse_of_recursion.pdf)
   - Authors: Ilia Shumailov, Zakhar Shumaylov, Yiren Zhao, Yarin Gal, Nicolas Papernot, Ross Anderson
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: `2305.17493`
   - Why relevant: Foundational model-collapse paper; formalizes tail-loss and recursive synthetic training failure.

2. [Self-Consuming Generative Models Go MAD](2307.01850_self_consuming_models_go_mad.pdf)
   - Authors: Sina Alemohammad, Josue Casco-Rodriguez, Lorenzo Luzi, Ahmed Imtiaz Humayun, Hossein Babaei, Daniel LeJeune, Ali Siahkoohi, Richard G. Baraniuk
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: `2307.01850`
   - Why relevant: Empirical collapse study across multiple generate-train loop regimes; useful baseline taxonomy.

3. [Large Language Models Suffer From Their Own Output: An Analysis of the Self-Consuming Training Loop](2311.16822_llms_suffer_from_their_own_output.pdf)
   - Authors: Martin Briesch, Dominik Sobania, Franz Rothlauf
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: `2311.16822`
   - Why relevant: Direct LLM evidence that recursive self-training preserves correctness longer than diversity.

   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: `2402.07087`

5. [Is Model Collapse Inevitable? Breaking the Curse of Recursion by Accumulating Real and Synthetic Data](2404.01413_is_model_collapse_inevitable.pdf)
