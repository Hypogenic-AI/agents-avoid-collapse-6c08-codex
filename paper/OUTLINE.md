# Outline: Recursive LLM Ecosystems Paper

## Title
- Recursive LLM Ecosystems Show an Inheritance Shock, Not a Simple Minimum Viable Population Threshold

## Abstract
- Motivate recursive synthetic-data ecosystems and the minimum viable population question.
- State the gap: prior collapse work studies single loops, not populations with operational individuality.
- Summarize proxy method: six conditions, four generations, replacement vs accumulation, individuality assay.
- Preview main findings with numbers: generation-1 truthfulness shock, best and worst final conditions, positive correlation between individuality and collapse score.
- Conclude that inheritance quality matters more than nominal population count in this setup.

## Introduction
- Hook: recursive synthetic text is already entering corpora, code, and dialogue.
- Explain why silent diversity decay matters even when headline quality remains acceptable.
- Identify literature gap: no population-level threshold study and no operational definition of an individual.
- Introduce our proxy recursive inheritance experiment and method figure.
- Quantitative preview of final results and correlation result.
- Contributions:
  - We define behavioral individuality via between-agent vs within-agent distance.
  - We conduct a population and regime sweep over recursive inheritance conditions.
  - We show a generation-1 truthfulness shock with partial recovery rather than monotonic collapse.
  - We show prompt-level role diversity is measurable but not protective in this setup.

## Related Work
